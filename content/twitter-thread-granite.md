🧵 1/10 Bought a $4K granite counter last year. Got hairline cracks 3 months in. Here's the 7-point checklist I wish I had:

2/10 Quarry name in invoice. 'Brazilian granite' is meaningless — there are 200+ quarries with wildly different quality.

3/10 Photo of YOUR slab before cutting. Not the showroom sample. Sellers swap pieces all the time.

4/10 Min 3cm thickness for counters. 2cm = cracks within 2 years. Non-negotiable.

5/10 Polish test: fingernail across the surface. If it catches anywhere, polish is bad.

6/10 Radiological certificate for indoor use. Some granites have elevated radon. Sellers won't mention this.

7/10 Beveled edges, not sharp 90°. Sharp corners WILL chip during transport. Every time.

8/10 25+ year warranty in writing. If they won't sign for it, they don't trust their own product.

9/10 Showroom samples are the #1 scam. The actual slab arrives weeks later. Always different. Demand photos of YOUR piece.

10/10 Save this thread before your next stone purchase. Repost to save someone $4K. 🪨
